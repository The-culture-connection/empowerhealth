6. Know Your Rights – Calm, Clear, Actionable
This screen is the moral center of EmpowerHealth Watch. It must educate without alarming, empower without escalating, and support without replacing medical or legal advice.

Emotional Goal
I understand my rights, and I feel confident advocating for myself.
If a user feels scared after reading this screen, it needs to be softened.

Core Positioning
This is education, not legal advice
This is about communication and consent, not confrontation

Generous spacing for readability

Ohio Provider Directory – Design & Data Rationale
Overview
As part of building the EmpowerHealth Watch provider directory, we are implementing a system that:
Includes all providers in Ohio (all ethnicities)
Allows users to filter by race and ethnicity
Supports a community-powered “Mama Approved™” trust system
Allows users to add providers (Yelp-style)
Maintains transparency, moderation controls, and data integrity
The goal is to balance inclusion, safety, legal awareness, and usability.

Why We Are Not Using a Simple “Race” Field
Most public provider directories (NPI registry, Medicaid directories, etc.) do not include structured race or ethnicity data.
Additionally, storing race/ethnicity as a single hard-coded field presents risks:
It may not be self-identified.
It may be incorrect or disputed.
It may create compliance or legal concerns.
Instead, we are using a tag-based identity system with verification tracking.
This allows:
Filtering by race/ethnicity
Transparency about how identity was added
Moderation if disputed
Community input without making unverified claims

1️⃣ Base Provider Dataset
We will seed the directory with Ohio providers from:
Medicaid directories
NPI registry
Other reputable provider databases  2024-2025 BIPOC MBH DIRECTORY_3rd Edition.pdf
This ensures:
Broad coverage
All ethnicities included
No exclusion at the system level

2️⃣ Identity Tags (Race / Ethnicity / Language)
Rather than a single “race” column, we use:

This stores filterable options such as:

Provider Identity Claims Table

Optional source URL
This allows the app to:

Legal & Ethical Positioning
Important principles guiding the structure:
Identity tags are not assumed; they are sourced and verifiable.
No race/ethnicity claim is presented as fact without attribution.
Users can report incorrect information.
Moderation logs and audit trails are maintained.
The system prioritizes transparency over assumption.
This reduces liability while preserving the ability to filter meaningfully.

EmpowerHealth Watch is not building a generic provider directory.
We are building:
A trust-based, community-informed provider ecosystem
A culturally affirming search experience
A scalable and defensible system
The schema supports growth beyond Ohio while maintaining structural integrity.
